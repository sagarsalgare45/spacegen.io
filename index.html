Introduction to Space Biology

Space biology delves into how living organisms respond to
the extreme conditions of space, such as microgravity,
cosmic radiation, and isolation. These unique environments
challenge biological processes like growth, reproduction,
and cellular function. By studying these effects, scientists
gain valuable knowledge that not only enhances our
understanding of human health in space but also leads
to advancements in medicine, agriculture, and biotechnology
on Earth.Insights from space biology research contribute
to safer, longer-term space missions, including the potential
colonization of other planets, and have applications in areas
such as disease treatment and environmental sustainability.
